◆ 02 — Editorial Philosophy

Start with something worth saying.

Content starts long before you open Canva. The question isn't "what should we post?" — it's "what's worth talking about?"

Before format
Story.

Tell a story first, let the format emerge. A format chosen before you have something to say produces content that feels like content.

Before information
Perspective.

Facts are everywhere. The Pynk Nymph point of view on those facts is what earns attention. "Waxing tips" is information. A perspective is content.

Before promotion
Recommendation.

Promote and people tune out. Genuinely recommend something — a service, a product, an org — and they lean in. The difference is intent.

Before announcement
Observation.

Announcements tell people what happened. Observations make them feel something. Lead with the human moment, then give them the information.

Before engagement
Trust.

Engagement is a byproduct of trust. The content that generates the most engagement earned it — not content designed to bait a response.

The test
Does it make someone more likely to trust us?

Even if they don't book today. That's the north star every principle points toward.

◆ 02 — Barriers to Booking

Five categories of doubt.

People don't book because they saw a discount. They book because something finally removed the last objection.

◆ Barrier 01 · Trust

Are they any good?

  • Are they skilled?
  • Are they experienced?
  • Do they know what they're talking about?
◆ Barrier 02 · Comfort

Will this be weird?

  • Will I be judged?
  • Will this be awkward?
  • Who is going to see me naked?
◆ Barrier 03 · Belonging

Is this place for me?

  • Will I fit in here?
  • Are they really inclusive?
  • What does "inclusive" actually mean?
◆ Barrier 04 · Logistics

Is this going to be annoying?

  • Where do I park?
  • How do I prepare?
  • What do I wear?

◆ Out in the Wild — official recurring series

Not an event calendar.
A point of view.

Each carousel tells a story. Slides play a role — not just fill a list position. The curation is the content; our perspective is why someone follows instead of just Googling.

Slide 01LearnHistory, context, or education about the moment or theme
Slide 02CelebrateAn org or event worth showing up for

◆ Visual standard

Use real photography. If highlighting another organization, let them be the hero — Pynk Nymph is the host. Avoid event flyers as the primary visual. Avoid AI-generated imagery for community posts.
