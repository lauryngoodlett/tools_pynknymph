Sound Like Us

  • Say "we" and "the team" — not "I" or just Hannah.
  • Richmond first. We're not a generic waxing studio — we're a Richmond studio.
  • Write like a real person, not a brand. If it sounds like a spa wrote it, rewrite it.
  • Funny is allowed. Weird is allowed. A little feral is on-brand.
  • Body-first, always. No euphemisms, no shame, no "smooth skin awaits you."
  • Direct over polished. "Get your ass waxed" over "Book your next appointment today."
  • Opinions are welcome. "We need to talk about…" is a valid opening.
  • Cheeky and warm — never clinical, never try-hard.
