◆ 04 — Voice & Tone

How we talk.

Four principles, each one a rule. The left column shows the behavior — the right shows what we'd never write. Hover to reveal.

Challenge assumptions

Hair is normal. Waxing is optional.

Lead with respect, not insecurity. We don't sell a problem — we offer a choice.

Never

"Show off that silky smooth skin."

Implies hair is a flaw to fix. That's the opposite of what we stand for.

← hover to reveal

Be direct

Get your ass waxed.

Say it plainly. Funny, unapologetic, ours. If it sounds like a spa brochure, rewrite it.

Never

"Schedule your rejuvenating treatment today."

This is every other spa in the country. We are not every other spa.

← hover to reveal

Say the quiet part out loud

Will you be judged? No.

Answer the question people are too embarrassed to ask. Don't hint at the reassurance — state it clearly.

Never

"We pride ourselves on a welcoming environment for all guests."

Corporate speak. Nobody reads it. Nobody believes it.

← hover to reveal

Talk like a human

First wax? We'll walk you through it.

Sound like a person, not a brand. Practical, warm, no flourishes — if it sounds polished, it's probably wrong.

Never

"Experience the ultimate luxury waxing journey."

Corporate PR speak. Nobody believes it.

◆ Do

◆ Don't

No drop shadows, gradients, or outlines on top. Don't stretch, rotate, or crowd them together.

◆ 07 — Grain & Gradient

Perfect isn't
the goal.

Human is. We use grain and gradient to keep the brand from feeling clinical, sterile, or corporate. We don't chase perfection — we chase comfort.

◆ Grain

Warm & tactile.

◆ Gradient

Energy & depth.

◆ Grain
  • Adds warmth and texture
  • Makes digital content feel tactile and lived-in
  • Balances clean layouts with personality
  • Use subtly — felt more than noticed
◆ Gradient
  • Adds energy, movement, and emotion
  • Creates depth without heavy illustration
  • Built from our pinks, greens, and warm neutrals
  • Should support content, not compete with it
× Avoid
  • Flat corporate perfection
  • Sterile beauty-brand minimalism
  • Heavy grain that hurts readability
  • Loud gradients that overpower the message

One sentence explains the whole visual world: body hair in photography, the grain, the gradients, human-sounding copy, imperfect illustrations, real community photography — all of it chases comfort over perfection.
