◆ 01 — Content Principles

The rules.

Five principles. Every photo and video has to pass at least one of them.

01.

Show real bodies.

Not one body, edited five ways. The full range of people who actually walk into the studio.

Different body types Different skin tones Different ages Different gender expressions
  • Real clients of every body type
  • A range of skin tones and ages
  • Different gender expressions
  • People who actually walk into the studio
  • One model edited five ways
  • A single “ideal” body standard
  • Tokenized or one-off diversity
  • Generic stock-photo casting
02.

Body hair is not the enemy.

This is our sharpest differentiator. Most of the category quietly says "hair is gross." We refuse to.

Body hair can appear Stubble is fine All growth stages OK Never dirty, never embarrassing
  • Visible body hair
  • Stubble and every growth stage
  • Hair shown as neutral and normal
  • Bodies exactly as they show up
  • “Hair is gross” framing
  • Only post-wax perfection
  • Shame-based before / afters
  • Calling hair dirty or embarrassing
03.

Real over perfect.

Texture, pores, and honest light. The second an image looks airbrushed, it stops being us.

  • Real clients
  • Real skin texture
  • Real lighting
  • Real moments
  • Excessive smoothing
  • Plastic-looking skin
  • Airbrushed beauty campaigns
  • Generic stock spa photography
04.

We are not a luxury spa.

We give the same level of care as any high-end spa — we're just real about it. The content should feel like the room, not a hotel brochure.

  • Personality & humor
  • Community
  • Richmond
  • Behind-the-scenes
  • White robes
  • Cucumber water
  • Zen stones
  • Generic spa stock photos
05.

Video style.

Same soul as the photos, in motion. Talk to people, don't perform at them.

  • Casual
  • Conversational
  • Educational
  • Process-oriented
  • Overproduced commercials
  • Forced trend-chasing
  • Filtered to oblivion
  • Background music that sounds like a spa
06.

Community visual standards.

When we highlight an organization, place, or event — the visual rules change. Pynk Nymph is the host. The subject is the hero.

  • Real photography from the org or event
  • Authentic moments, not staged or stock shots
  • The organization's own imagery (with credit)
  • Candid community moments over posed ones
  • Event flyers as the primary visual
  • AI-generated imagery for community posts
  • Generic stock photography of community scenes
  • Pynk Nymph branding that overshadows the subject

◆ Why flyers don't lead

Photography tells stories. Flyers communicate information. A flyer answers who, what, when, where — but doesn't make anyone feel anything. Use flyers as supplemental info in captions or later carousel slides; let real photography carry the opening frame.
